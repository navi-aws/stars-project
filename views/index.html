Stars don’t twinkle

Even though you can look outside at night and see the flashing of different levels of light emanating from the same star, what you’re really seeing is interference from the atmosphere making the stars look like they’re twinkling.

Stars Produce Light

Four hydrogen atoms combine into a helium atom and release energy, which is converted to light.
